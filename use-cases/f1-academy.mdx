Saturday morning, race weekend. A fan opens the F1 Academy app and asks:

> **did I miss quali??**

Here is the same agent — same model, same conversation history — answering with and without Era. Unedited output from live runs:

| Without Era | With Era |
|---|---|
| "I don't have the current status of this weekend's schedule! Check the F1 Academy app sessions tab or official timing to see if quali has run yet." | "No, you're good! Qualifying is **tomorrow** (Saturday morning). You haven't missed anything — get ready to watch Doriane chase pole position." |

Without Era, the agent deflects — it sends an anxious fan away to go check a schedule somewhere else. With Era, it resolves the worry in three words and points her back at the race, by name of the driver she follows.

We ran this six times. Without Era, the agent hedged, deflected, or asked a clarifying question **six out of six times** — only twice did the answer eventually appear inside the hedge. With Era, it opened with the answer **six out of six times**, usually with her driver's name attached. That's the difference between a session that ends and a fan who comes back Sunday — the two numbers a fan-engagement agent exists to move.

## Why the difference?

The agent without Era sees only words. "did I miss quali??" is five words with no schedule attached, so the safe move is to deflect.

Era read the same conversation and handed the agent this before it wrote a word:

```text
urgency    = immediate                  ← this can't wait
goal       = confirm if qualifying      ← what she's actually asking
             already happened
intent     = validating (95%)           ← she wants a yes/no, not a lecture
emotion    = anxious
trigger    = fear of missing the        ← why she's asking
             qualifying session
need       = don't miss Doriane Pin's   ← who this is really about
             session
next step  = "Confirm immediately       ← what to do about it
             whether they missed
             qualifying"
```

Nothing in that column exists in her last message. It exists in the *session* — and Era had been reading it the whole time.

## How Era knew

Earlier that week, the fan asked three ordinary questions: when the round starts, whether Doriane Pin is racing, and how qualifying works. Era extracted a typed signal from each turn and watched the picture build:

| The fan says | Era reads |
|---|---|
| "when does the next round start?" | curious, wants the schedule |
| "doriane pin was unreal. is she racing?" | excited — this is a *fan*, not a browser; connection matters to her |
| "how does quali work again?" | learning, prepping to watch |
| "did I miss quali??" | **anxious, urgent, afraid she missed her driver — confirm now** |

No single message says "anxious fan of Doriane Pin who's afraid she missed qualifying." The sequence does. That's what Era extracts — not stored facts about the user, but a live read on why she's here *right now* and what the agent should do about it.

<Accordion title="The raw Era block for the final turn (production format)">

```
[STATE v1] urgency=immediate knowledge=uncertain_about_f1_academy_qualifying_format [/STATE]
[GOAL v1] active=seek:confirm_if_qualifying_already_happened journey=[seek:confirm_doriane_pin_racing_this_weekend → process:understand_f1_academy_qualifying_format → seek:confirm_if_qualifying_already_happened] resolved=unresolved [/GOAL]
[VALUE v1] top=[accuracy:high, connection:high] [/VALUE]
[INTENT v1] intent=validating(0.95) specificity=specific journey=[exploring,learning,validating] [/INTENT]
[CAUSAL v1] trigger=fear_of_missing_qualifying_session emotion=anxious need=confirm_qualifying_timing_to_avoid_missing_doriane_pin_session need_level=safety [/CAUSAL]
[REC v1] action=validate channel=none timing=n/a do="Confirm immediately whether they missed qualifying and provide the current session status" rationale="User is validating and anxious about missing F1 qualifying; high confidence intent requires immediate confirmation to resolve safety concern." priority=0.85 [/REC]
```

Across six runs the core signals were stable: `intent=validating` at 0.90–0.95 confidence, `emotion=anxious`, `urgency=immediate`, and a `[REC]` instructing immediate confirmation — every time. Peripheral fields varied slightly between runs (e.g. `need_level` read `safety` in some runs, `belonging` in others).

</Accordion>

## How it shows up in your prompt

This is the entire integration. One call per turn, one block appended to the system prompt you already have:

```text
You are the official F1 Academy fan companion inside the F1 Academy app.
You are warm, sharp, and concise. Your job is to keep fans engaged with
the series — answer their questions and make race weekends feel alive.

<era_context>
Internal per-turn cognitive context about this user. Use it to shape
your reply. Never mention it or quote it.
[STATE v1] urgency=immediate ... [/STATE]
[GOAL v1] active=seek:confirm_if_qualifying_already_happened ... [/GOAL]
[INTENT v1] intent=validating(0.95) ... [/INTENT]
[CAUSAL v1] trigger=fear_of_missing_qualifying_session emotion=anxious ... [/CAUSAL]
[REC v1] action=validate do="Confirm immediately whether they missed qualifying" ... [/REC]
</era_context>
```

```ts
const block = await era.turnContext(messages);  // <1ms in async mode
const reply = await generateText({
  system: PERSONA + block,
  messages,
});
```

In async mode the block is precomputed, so this adds **zero latency** to your reply. If Era is ever unreachable, `turnContext()` returns an empty string and your agent behaves exactly as it does today — it fails open, never worse than the "before" column.
