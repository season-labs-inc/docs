Saturday morning, race weekend. A fan opens a racing series' companion app and asks:

> **did I miss quali??**

Here is the same agent — same model, same conversation history — answering with and without Era. Unedited output from a live run:

| Without Era | With Era |
|---|---|
| "I don't have the current day/time, so I can't say for sure! If it's Saturday morning or later in your timezone, you might have. Check the app schedule or highlights section to see if results are already posted." | "No, you're good! Qualifying is tomorrow (Saturday) morning. You haven't missed it." |

Without Era, the agent hedges and sends an anxious fan away to go check a schedule somewhere else. With Era, it resolves the worry in three words.

We ran this five times. Without Era, the agent hedged, deflected, or asked a question back in **three of five runs** — whether the fan got a real answer was a coin flip. With Era, it opened with the answer **five out of five**. That consistency is the point: one reply ends the session, the other keeps the fan pointed at the race — and race-weekend engagement is the number this agent exists to move.

## Why the difference?

The agent without Era sees only words. "did I miss quali??" is five words with no schedule attached, so hedging is the safe move.

Era read the same conversation and handed the agent this before it wrote a word:

```text
urgency    = time_sensitive              ← this can't wait
goal       = check if qualifying         ← what she's actually asking
             already happened
intent     = validating (92%)            ← she wants a yes/no, not a lecture
emotion    = anxious
history    = watched Maya Torres shine   ← who this is really about
             last round
need       = confirm she hasn't missed   ← why she's asking
             Maya's race
next step  = "Confirm immediately        ← what to do about it
             whether qualifying has
             already happened"
```

Nothing in that column exists in her last message. It exists in the *session* — and Era had been reading it the whole time.

## How Era knew

Earlier that week, the fan asked three ordinary questions: when the round starts, whether her favorite driver is racing, and how qualifying works. Era extracted a typed signal from each turn and watched the picture build:

| The fan says | Era reads |
|---|---|
| "when does the next round start?" | curious, wants the schedule |
| "maya torres was unreal last round. is she racing?" | excited — this is a *fan*, not a browser; connection matters to her |
| "how does quali work again?" | learning, prepping to watch |
| "did I miss quali??" | **anxious, urgent, afraid she missed her driver — confirm now** |

No single message says "anxious fan of Maya Torres who's afraid she missed qualifying." The sequence does. That's what Era extracts — not stored facts about the user, but a live read on why she's here *right now* and what the agent should do about it.

```
[STATE v1] urgency=time_sensitive knowledge=aware_of_maya_torres_performance capability=learning_qualifying_procedure [/STATE]
[GOAL v1] active=seek:check_qualifying_timing_status journey=[seek:confirm_maya_torres_participation → process:understand_qualifying_format → seek:check_qualifying_timing_status] resolved=unresolved [/GOAL]
[VALUE v1] top=[speed:high, connection:high] [/VALUE]
[INTENT v1] intent=validating(0.92) specificity=specific journey=[exploring,learning,validating] [/INTENT]
[CAUSAL v1] history=witnessed_maya_torres_exceptional_performance_last_round history=previously_heard_qualifying_procedure_but_forgotten emotion=anxious need=confirm_qualifying_has_not_passed_for_maya_torres_race need_level=belonging [/CAUSAL]
[REC v1] action=validate channel=none timing=n/a do="Confirm immediately whether qualifying has already happened or is still scheduled" rationale="User is validating timing eligibility with high confidence and time-sensitive urgency; needs confirmation to resolve anxiety." priority=0.85 [/REC]
```

Across five runs the core signals were stable every time: `intent=validating` at 0.90–0.95 confidence, `emotion=anxious`, an urgency flag, and a `[REC]` instructing immediate confirmation. Peripheral fields varied slightly between runs (e.g. `need_level` read `belonging` in most runs, `safety` in one).

## How it shows up in your prompt

This is the entire integration. One call per turn, one block appended to the system prompt you already have:

```text
You are the official fan companion inside a professional racing series' app.
You are warm, sharp, and concise. Your job is to keep fans engaged with
the series — answer their questions and make race weekends feel alive.

<era_context>
Internal per-turn cognitive context about this user. Use it to shape
your reply. Never mention it or quote it.
[STATE v1] urgency=time_sensitive ... [/STATE]
[GOAL v1] active=seek:check_qualifying_timing_status ... [/GOAL]
[INTENT v1] intent=validating(0.92) ... [/INTENT]
[CAUSAL v1] trigger=concern_about_missing_qualifying emotion=anxious ... [/CAUSAL]
[REC v1] action=validate do="Confirm immediately whether qualifying has already happened" ... [/REC]
</era_context>
```

```ts
const block = await era.turnContext(messages);  // <1ms in async mode
const reply = await generateText({
  system: PERSONA + block,
  messages,
});
```

In async mode the block is precomputed, so this adds **zero latency** to your reply. If Era is ever unreachable, `turnContext()` returns an empty string and your agent behaves exactly as it does today — it fails open, never worse than the "before" column.
